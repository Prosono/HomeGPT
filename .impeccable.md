## Design Context

### Users
HomeGPT is used by end users of a smart home system who want practical insight into what is happening in the home without digging through raw Home Assistant data. The UI should help them understand events, energy usage, temperature patterns, history, and what can be improved, fixed, optimized, or watched more closely.

### Brand Personality
Premium, elegant, and structured. The interface should feel calm, trustworthy, and refined rather than flashy or experimental. It should help users feel in control of their home, with clear next steps and strong visual order.

### Aesthetic Direction
Build a dual-theme interface with both light and dark modes. Favor a premium editorial control-room feel with strong hierarchy, generous spacing, warm-neutral surfaces, and a restrained accent palette. The UI should avoid noisy dashboard clichés, overdone glassmorphism, generic AI gradients, and cluttered card grids.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with clarity: the most valuable home insights should surface first.
2. Keep the interface calm: use strong hierarchy and quiet visual rhythm instead of visual noise.
3. Make energy and comfort legible: charts, heatmaps, and history should be easy to scan and compare.
4. Write for end users: labels and explanations should be plain, specific, and actionable.
5. Treat every surface as a decision tool: each section should help the user understand, decide, or act.
